Biography

I am an Associate Reseacher at the School of Computer Science and Technology, USTC. I am also affiliated with the State Key Laboratory of Cognitive Intelligence at the side of USTC, working under the leadership of Prof. Enhong Chen and Prof. Qi Liu. Previously, I obtained my Ph.D. degree under the supervision of Prof. Qi Liu .

Research Interests

My research focuses on structured data analysis (e.g., time series, spatio-temporal, and tabular data) and large language model (LLM)-based reasoning and agent, with an emphasis on context-aware forecasting, particularly in AI for Science and Recommender Systems.

  • Time Series and Tabular Data Mining: We develop core methods for time series and tabular data, focusing on forecasting, classification, anomaly detection. Our interests include time series representation, contextual reasoning, agentic forecasting, and the development of foundation models for universal structured data representation.
  • LLM-based Reasoning and Agent: We aim to enhance LLM via training-free contextual learning and reinforcement learning, focusing on long CoT reasoning, planning, long-context memory, tool use, self-reflection, and multi-agent collaboration. Our objective is to build agents that can perceive, reason, and act autonomously in complex environments.
  • AI for Science: We focus on two directions: (1) understanding structured scientific data, such as time series and tabular data; and (2) building intelligent agents for scientific reasoning, with abilities in inference, planning, and tool use. Our goal is to accelerate discovery by combining data mining and LLM-driven reasoning within autonomous agent systems.
  • Recommender Systems: Advance personalized recommendation through sequential and spatio-temporal modeling and one-class collaborative filtering. We investigate reinforcement learning-based methods and leverage large language models to enable context-aware recommendation agents. Our goal is to build generalizable frameworks for dynamic, real-world environments.
