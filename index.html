Biography

I am an Associate Reseacher at the School of Computer Science and Technology, USTC. I am also affiliated with the State Key Laboratory of Cognitive Intelligence at the side of USTC, working under the leadership of Prof. Enhong Chen and Prof. Qi Liu. Previously, I obtained my Ph.D. degree under the supervision of Prof. Qi Liu .

Research Interests

My primary research interests focus on data mining and machine learning algorithms for time series and sequence data, with applications in biomedical big data and recommender systems. I am dedicated to developing effective and transferable machine learning algorithms that prioritize not only generalizability but also trustworthiness, with an emphasis on robustness, privacy preservation, and interpretability.

  • Time Series Analysis: Specializing in building time series and sequence modeling algorithms from low-level preprocessing and data representation to foundational modeling and the development of trustworthy machine learning algorithms tailored for multivariate time series.
  • Large Language Models (LLMs): Deeply committed to advancing the field of LLMs, with a particular focus on retrieval-augmented generation (RAG), LLM-based reasoning, LLM-driven agent techniques, and evaluation methods for multi-dimensional capabilities LLMs.
  • Biomedical Big Data: Specializing in biomedical big data analysis, covering the entire spectrum from clinical data—including human physiological signals (such as, ABP, EEG et al), electronic health records (EHR)—to high-throughput omics datasets.
  • Recommender Systems: Specializing in user behavior modeling, including sequential and session-based recommendation, one-class learning with implicit feedback, automated machine learning techniques (e.g., NAS) for RecSys, and transfer learning in recommendation.
